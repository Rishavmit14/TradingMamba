## LIQUIDITY

*Teaching time: 2.5 min | Words: 383 | Frames: 11*

### Liquidity — The First Market Driver

#### Definition
Liquidity is an area where stop losses and pending orders are resting in any market — forex, commodities, or any asset class. The teacher states that liquidity acts as a "driving force" for the market and is one of the two major market drivers (alongside inducement). Price is attracted to liquidity pools because institutions need to fill their large orders.

#### Types of Liquidity
1. **Pending Orders**: Buy Stops, Buy Limits, Sell Stops, Sell Limits
2. **Stop Losses**: Protective orders placed by traders
3. **Breakeven Levels**: Where traders move stop losses to protect profit
4. **Equal Highs / Double Top**: Retail marks as resistance — buy stops and sell limits accumulate above
5. **Equal Lows / Double Bottom**: Retail marks as support — sell stops and buy limits accumulate below
6. **Trendline-based Liquidity**: Orders clustered along retail trendlines

#### How Market Uses Liquidity
Demonstrated with a schematic diagram: breakout traders set buy stops above equal highs, reversal traders set sell limits. On the flip side, breakout traders set sell stops below equal lows. When market moves, it sweeps these orders. What retail calls "manipulation" is actually a liquidity sweep.

#### Trading Application
- Do NOT be part of liquidity — understand the smart money move first
- Recognize where liquidity pools exist
- Expect price to sweep these levels before making the real move

## INDUCEMENT

*Teaching time: 2.8 min | Words: 477 | Frames: 11*

### Inducement — The Second Market Driver (70% of Forex)

#### Definition
Inducement is a trap before an area of Order Block, Supply, or Demand. Price induces impatient buyers/sellers into the market before the actual zone is met, to create liquidity. The teacher states 70% of the forex market works around inducement.

#### Identification
- Minor pullback or correction within a price swing
- Marked with "IDM" notation
- Found on the LEFT side of the current swing high
- A swing can have 0, 1, 2, 3, or more inducement levels

#### Single vs Multiple Swings (Critical)
If price does NOT take out any inducement and keeps rising, the entire move is a SINGLE price swing. New swings only start AFTER inducement is swept. Retail traders miscount swing legs because they don't understand this.

#### Inducement + Order Block Interaction
1. Price rises, creating inducement at the first pullback
2. Retail traders do "Early Buying" at the inducement (the trap)
3. They set breakeven and stop loss levels
4. Market drops deep into the Order Block below, sweeping all that liquidity
5. The real move begins from the Order Block

## ORDER BLOCK

*Teaching time: 1.0 min | Words: 155 | Frames: 5*

### Order Block — The True Entry Zone

#### Definition
An Order Block is a specific price zone where institutional orders are resting. In this video, introduced as the zone BELOW the inducement level where real buying/selling occurs. Smart money protects the Order Block from retail traders.

#### Relationship with Inducement
- Inducement sits ABOVE the Order Block — it's the trap
- Retail traders get induced to buy at the trap level
- When market resumes, price sweeps through inducement and dips into the Order Block
- The Order Block activates and the real move begins

## LIQUIDITY SWEEP

*Teaching time: 0.6 min | Words: 119 | Frames: 4*

### Liquidity Sweep — Not Manipulation

#### Definition
A Liquidity Sweep occurs when price moves through a previous high/low, sweeping resting orders, but the candle body does NOT close above the high. This distinguishes it from a valid breakout. The teacher states: what retail calls "manipulation" is actually a liquidity sweep.

#### Liquidity Sweep vs Valid Higher High
- Candle body closes above high AND below inducement = Valid HH
- Wick pierces above but body doesn't close properly = Liquidity Sweep
