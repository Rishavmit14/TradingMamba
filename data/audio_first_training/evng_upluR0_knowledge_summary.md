### 2. High Probability Inducement Selection

**The 3 Scenarios:**

| Scenario | Condition | Rule |
|----------|-----------|------|
| 1 | Single inducement exists | Use it (100% probability) |
| 2 | Multiple inducements, DIFFERENT sizes | Use LARGEST/DEEPEST pullback |
| 3 | Multiple inducements, SAME size | Use MOST RECENT inducement |

**Logic:**
- **Scenario 2:** Larger pullback = Larger liquidity pool = Higher Smart Money priority
- **Scenario 3:** Recent inducement = Fresh liquidity = More active stops/orders

### 3. Inducement Size Priority

**Principle:**
Larger (deeper) pullbacks create bigger liquidity pools with more stop losses and pending orders.

**Why Size Matters:**
1. Larger retracement = More retail traders caught
2. More stops accumulated = Bigger liquidity pool
3. Smart Money prioritizes larger liquidity pools
4. Larger inducement gets taken out first

**Measurement:**
- Size = Depth of price retracement (not time duration)
- Compare pullback depths to identify largest

---

### 4. Inducement Recency Priority

**When Applied:**
Only when multiple inducements have the SAME size

**Logic:**
- Most recent = Closest to current high/low
- Recent liquidity is FRESH (active stops/orders)
- Older inducements may have stale orders
- Fresh liquidity is higher priority for Smart Money

**Tiebreaker:**
This is the tiebreaker rule when size cannot differentiate

### 6. Trendline Liquidity

**Retail Approach:**
1. Draw trendlines connecting swing lows (uptrend) or highs (downtrend)
2. Place trades at trendline touches
3. Place stops just beyond trendline

**Smart Money Response:**
1. Identify trendline liquidity concentrations
2. Break trendline to hunt stops
3. Resume trend direction after collecting liquidity

**Trading Rule:**
- Trendline breaks are often liquidity hunts, NOT trend reversals
- Don't place stops at obvious trendline levels
- Wait for trendline violation + reentry for confirmation

### 7. Support & Resistance Liquidity

**Retail Belief:**
Price will bounce at horizontal S/R levels

**Retail Action:**
Place stops just beyond S/R levels

**Smart Money Action:**
1. Break S/R to trigger stops
2. Collect liquidity
3. Reverse in expected direction

**Result:**
Retail traders stopped out before the expected move happens

**Key Rule:**
S/R violations are liquidity hunts, not trend changes
