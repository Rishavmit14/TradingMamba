## Concepts Taught

### 1. Millions Dollar Setup (Complete Intraday Trading Strategy)

The core concept of this video. A complete ICT-based intraday strategy combining liquidity sweep, expansion, market structure shift (MSS), fair value gap (FVG), and inducement into a single sequential checklist. The strategy applies identically to both sell (bearish) and buy (bullish) setups.

**Strategy Roadmap (Six Prerequisites):**
1. Structure Mapping (Trend Identification)
2. Price Cycle (Price Delivery -- consolidation, expansion, retracement, reversal)
3. Price Imbalance (Liquidity Void / FVG)
4. Entry Zones (Order Blocks / Buy-Sell Zones)
5. Setup Confirmation (Liquidity & MSS)
6. Risk Reward Management (SL/TP)

**The 7-Step Checklist (Bearish Sell Setup):**
1. Liquidity Sweep/Grab above a short-term high
2. Expansion / Sharp Move downward
3. Price closes below the previous (middle) low
4. Market Structure Shift (MSS) confirmed
5. Liquidity Void / FVG created in the expansion
6. Inducement level created (internal liquidity trap)
7. Wait for price to tap the entry zone (FVG/order block)

The bullish buy setup mirrors all rules: liquidity swept below support, expansion upward, MSS confirmed above previous high, FVG in expansion, inducement, and buy zone entry.

### 2. Liquidity Sweep (Liquidity Grab)

The first and starting condition for the setup. Price takes out resting orders (stop losses, breakout entries) accumulated above resistance or below support. Marked as "Liquidity Taken Out (X)" with "$$$" symbols on schematic diagrams. The sweep reveals the direction of institutional intent: institutions use the swept liquidity to fill opposing orders.

- For sells: sweep above resistance (buy-side liquidity)
- For buys: sweep below support (sell-side liquidity)
- Without a liquidity sweep, no valid setup exists

---

### 3. Market Structure Shift (MSS)

A critical distinction taught in this video: MSS is explicitly NOT the same as Break of Structure (BOS). MSS is a triple-condition event requiring:
1. Liquidity grab first
2. Expansion phase
3. Price body closing beyond the previous swing point

The instructor provides a text annotation on the real chart example: "MSS is not BOS. MSS is When Market Take Out Liquidity and After Expansion, Price Closed Below Previous (Middle) Low."

MSS confirms institutional commitment to the new direction and validates the setup.

---

### 4. Expansion Phase

The sharp, aggressive price move after the liquidity sweep, representing the "footprint of smart money." When institutions distribute orders, the resulting move is fast and unidirectional. The expansion must break through the previous swing to create the MSS, and it typically creates an FVG due to the speed of the move. Visually labeled with "Expansion" vertically on schematics and highlighted as shaded zones on real charts.

---

### 5. Inducement (The Missing Block)

Disclosed in this lecture as the previously missing element from the strategy. Inducement is a short-term counter-trend move after MSS that traps retail traders, creating internal liquidity for institutions. Key teaching: "If any setup has an inducement, this will be high probability setup." The inducement level is the final confirmation before the entry zone becomes valid. Setups without inducement are lower probability.

---

### 6. Fair Value Gap / Liquidity Void (Within the Setup)

The FVG created during the expansion phase serves as the primary entry zone. The fifth checklist item requires: "Expansion phase must have a liquidity void." The FVG zone sits between the MSS level and the liquidity sweep level. Entry orders (sell limit or buy limit) are placed at or within this zone. Entry validity requires both the FVG existence AND a subsequent inducement.

---

### 7. Risk Reward Management

The final roadmap component. The preferred ratio is 1:3 (risk to reward). Stop loss goes beyond the liquidity sweep level; take profit is at least 3x the risk distance in the institutional direction. Described as the most important part of trading, especially for prop firm / funded trader evaluation accounts.

## Key Distinctions Made

- **MSS vs BOS**: MSS requires liquidity grab + expansion + close beyond previous swing. BOS is a simple structural break. This distinction is critical for filtering false signals.
- **Inducement as the "Missing Block"**: Previous lectures covered liquidity, expansion, and MSS, but inducement was the undisclosed element that makes setups high probability.
- **Sequential Checklist**: All 7 conditions must be met in order. Partial setups are invalid.
