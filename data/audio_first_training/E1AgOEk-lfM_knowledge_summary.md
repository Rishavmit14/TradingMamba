### 2. Inducement Shift - Rule 1

**Condition:** Market did NOT sweep current inducement AND created a new high/low

**Action:** Inducement SHIFTS to new valid pullback (first valid pullback on left side of new high/low)

**Example:**
1. Market has inducement at previous pullback
2. Price creates NEW high WITHOUT sweeping old inducement
3. Old inducement becomes obsolete
4. NEW inducement = first valid pullback on left of new high

**Key Insight:** Must constantly update which inducement level is currently active.

---

### 3. Inducement Shift - Rule 2

**Condition:** Market DID sweep inducement BUT candle did NOT close below it (wick-only sweep) AND created new high/low

**Action:** The SWEEP SWING also acts as inducement

**Result:** TWO inducement levels exist simultaneously:
1. The sweep swing low (where wick touched)
2. First valid pullback on left side of new high

**Trading Implications:**
- Market may target EITHER inducement
- Market may target BOTH inducements before true move
- Choice depends on order block location and price imbalances
- If bullish OB is deeper, market sweeps both inducements to reach actual OB

### 4. Inducement Shift - Rule 3 (MOST CRITICAL)

**THE BIGGEST SECRET IN SMART MONEY TRADING**

**Condition:** Market DID sweep inducement AND candle closed below it AND created new high/low with an IMPULSE (without pullback)

**Action:** The LOW of this impulse swing acts as INDUCEMENT (NOT as valid Higher Low)

**Impulse Swing Definition:** Move from low to high WITHOUT any pullback in between

**Critical Insight:**
- When swing forms via impulse (no pullback), the low = inducement
- Do NOT mark this as Higher Low or key support level
- This is where retail traders get TRAPPED

**Why Most Critical:**
- Most retail and conventional SMC traders get stuck here
- Gives true SMC traders an EDGE over retail
- Prevents becoming liquidity for Smart Money

### 5. Smart Money Trap - Impulse Swing

**The Trap Sequence:**

1. Market moves low to high in IMPULSE (no pullback)
2. Low looks like Higher Low to retail traders
3. Price creates Higher High, then breaks the "HL"
4. **Retail sees:** CHoCH (Change of Character) - starts SELLING
5. **Smart Money sees:** Inducement sweep - starts BUYING
6. Price reverses upward, trapping retail shorts
7. Continues bullish, liquidating retail stops

**Retail Perspective:**
- "Major Higher Low broken = market reversal"
- Action: Sell expecting downtrend
- Result: Trapped and stopped out

**Smart Money Perspective:**
- "HL formed via impulse = actually inducement"
- Action: Buy at inducement sweep (trend still bullish)
- Result: Profit from trend continuation

**How to Avoid:**
1. Always check HOW the Higher Low formed
2. If formed via IMPULSE (no pullback) → it's inducement
3. Don't mark as key support or valid HL
4. When price breaks it → inducement sweep (NOT CHoCH)
5. Look to BUY (in bullish trend) instead of SELL

## Three Rules Summary

| Rule | Condition | Action |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Rule 1** | Market did NOT sweep inducement + created new high/low | Inducement shifts to new valid pullback |

| **Rule 3** | Market DID sweep (closed below) + created new high/low via IMPULSE | Impulse swing low acts as inducement (NOT Higher Low) |
